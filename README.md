# **Access Safe Abortion Services in Illinois**

When you are facing an unplanned pregnancy, safety is likely your top concern. You want to know that the care you receive is safe, that the provider is qualified, and that you will be protected throughout the process. The good news is that Illinois has some of the strongest protections for abortion access in the country, and safe abortion services are available close to home.

This guide covers everything you need to know about accessing safe abortion services in Illinois, from verifying a provider's credentials to understanding your legal rights as a patient.

## **What Makes Abortion Services Safe?**

Safe abortion services share several key characteristics. The provider should be a licensed medical professional with specific training in abortion care. The facility should be clean, well-maintained, and equipped to handle emergencies. The medications used should be FDA-approved. The provider should offer follow-up care and an after-hours number for questions or concerns.

In Illinois, abortion is legal and regulated, which means clinics must meet the same safety standards as any other outpatient medical facility. The risk of serious complications from a safe, legal abortion is extremely low—lower than many common medical procedures including wisdom tooth extraction and tonsillectomy.

## **Explore Abortion Options Near You**

When exploring your abortion options, you will find two main types: medication abortion (the abortion pill) and procedural abortion (sometimes called surgical abortion).

**Medication abortion** is available for early pregnancy, typically up to 12 weeks. It involves taking two medications that work together to end the pregnancy. The process happens mostly at home, with support from your provider by phone or text. This option offers privacy and the ability to be in your own environment.

**Procedural abortion** is available throughout pregnancy. It is a quick in-clinic procedure performed by a licensed provider. The procedure itself takes only a few minutes. This option is completed during a single appointment and recovery is often faster.

Both options are safe, effective, and legal in Illinois. Your provider can help you decide which option is right for you based on how far along you are and your medical history.

To explore abortion options near you, schedule a consultation with the Matteson clinic, where providers will explain both options in detail.

## **How to Verify a Provider's Credentials**

Before choosing a provider, verify their credentials. You can do this by:

- Checking the clinic's website for information about their medical staff  
- Asking directly when you call:  
  *"Are your providers licensed physicians, nurse practitioners, or physician assistants?"*  
- Looking for reviews from other patients  
- Checking with state medical boards if you have concerns  

A reputable clinic will be transparent about their credentials. If a provider is evasive or refuses to answer, consider that a red flag.

Also verify that the clinic offers follow-up care. Safe abortion care does not end when you leave the appointment. You should have access to a provider for questions or concerns during your recovery.

## **Your Legal Rights in Illinois**

Illinois law protects your right to abortion. Unlike many other states, Illinois does not have:

- Mandatory waiting periods  
- Parental consent or notification requirements for minors  
- Gestational bans that cut off access early in pregnancy  

This means you can access care as soon as you are ready, without unnecessary delays. Your medical information is protected by privacy laws, and your provider cannot share information about your care without your permission.

If you have questions about your legal rights, ask when you call. The clinic staff can explain the laws that protect you.

## **What to Ask When You Call a Clinic**

When you call a clinic, ask these questions to ensure you are choosing a safe provider:

- How far along can I be for medication abortion? For procedural abortion?  
- Do you offer telehealth appointments?  
- What are your fees, and do you offer financial assistance?  
- Do you accept Medicaid?  
- How soon can I get an appointment?  
- Do you have evening or weekend hours?  
- What is your follow-up process?  
- Do you have an after-hours number for questions or concerns?  

A reputable clinic will answer these questions clearly and without hesitation. If a clinic is evasive or refuses to answer, find another provider.

## **The Importance of Follow-Up Care**

Follow-up care is an essential part of safe abortion services. Your provider should schedule a follow-up appointment or phone call to confirm that the abortion is complete and that you are healing properly.

For medication abortion, follow-up may involve a phone call and a pregnancy test. For procedural abortion, follow-up is typically brief, confirming that you have no signs of infection and that your body is healing.

If you have any concerns after your appointment, your provider should be available to answer questions. A reputable clinic will provide an after-hours number for emergencies or urgent concerns.

## **Telehealth for Safe Abortion Care**

Telehealth is a safe and effective option for eligible patients. Research has shown that telehealth abortion is just as safe as in-person care. The medications are the same. The medical screening is the same. The follow-up is the same. The only difference is that you are at home instead of in a clinic.

Telehealth removes barriers to access, making safe abortion care available to patients who live far from a clinic, have limited transportation, or prefer the privacy of their own home.

For Illinois residents who prefer virtual care, confidential abortion care services via telehealth are available from providers serving the south suburbs.

## **Financial Assistance for Safe Abortion Care**

Cost should never be a barrier to safe abortion care. Illinois Medicaid covers abortion services for eligible residents at no out-of-pocket cost. The clinic staff can help you determine if you qualify and assist with the application process.

For those who do not qualify for Medicaid, nonprofit abortion funds may be able to help cover the cost of care. Some funds also help with related expenses such as transportation, lodging, and childcare.

When you call to schedule, ask about financial assistance. The staff is there to help.
